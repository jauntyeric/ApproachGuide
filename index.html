The Approach Guide (2024)

Master the art of genuine conversation openers

The Five Elements:

  • The Hello: Simple greeting with eye contact
  • The Observation: Comment on something specific
  • The Why: Share your genuine reason for approaching
  • The Introduction: State your name clearly
  • The Lead: Open-ended question
Example:
"Hey! [wait for eye contact]
I noticed you checking out the bookshelf.
I'm a huge reader myself and wanted to say hi.
I'm Sarah.
What kind of books do you enjoy?"
Key Tips:
  • Wait for eye contact before continuing
  • Keep your body language open and relaxed
  • Speak clearly and at a good volume
  • Be genuine in your observation

Key Elements:

  • Situational Observation
  • Playful Comment or Question
  • Natural Transition
Examples:
  • "I wonder what the owner's favorite color is?" (in an overtly red bar)
  • "You'd make for a terrible ninja today!" (to someone with loud shoes)
  • "I think the bartender is trying to get me drunk! Is yours this strong?"
Key Tips:
  • Use the environment for natural conversation starters
  • Keep it light and playful
  • Allow for easy exit if they're not interested

Key Principles:

  • Wait for natural openings in the group's dynamic
  • Read the group's energy level
  • Address the group as a whole initially
Example:
"Hey everyone! I saw you all laughing over here and you looked like the most fun group at the event. I had to come say hi. I'm Eric. What are you all celebrating?"

Essential Elements:

  • Relaxed shoulders and open posture
  • Natural eye contact (70-80% while listening)
  • Appropriate distance (respect personal space)
  • Genuine smile
The Car Analogy:
  • High Beams: Eye contact intensity
  • Low Beams: Chest direction and openness
  • Horn: Voice tone and volume

If They're Not Receptive:

"I see. Well, I'm still glad I came over and said hi. I was drawn towards you for some reason. I hope you have a great evening."
Remember:
  • Exit gracefully
  • Maintain your composure
  • Keep it positive
  • Learn from each interaction
